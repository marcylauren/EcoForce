Land Pollution

Land pollution, also known as soil pollution, is the degradation of land quality due to human activities. Here are some key points about land pollution:


  • It occurs when hazardous chemicals, waste, or pollutants contaminate the soil, making it unsuitable for agriculture, vegetation, or human habitation.
  • Land pollution can result from industrial activities, improper waste disposal, mining, deforestation, and urbanization.
  • It poses serious threats to ecosystems, biodiversity, and public health, leading to soil erosion, water contamination, and loss of fertile land.
  • Preventing land pollution requires sustainable land management practices, waste reduction, reforestation, and remediation of contaminated sites.
